Privacy Policy

MagicCast Studio · Last updated: May 31, 2026

MagicCast Studio ("the App", "we", "us") is a personal content-automation tool that helps its operator create and publish short-form videos about Disney theme-park news to the operator's own connected social-media accounts (such as YouTube and TikTok). This policy explains what data the App accesses and how it is handled.

Who this applies to

The App is a single-operator tool. It acts only on the accounts of the person who installs it and authorizes access. It does not collect data from, or provide services to, members of the public.

Data we access

  • TikTok account information — via TikTok Login Kit, with your explicit authorization, the App reads basic profile information (your TikTok open ID and display name) solely to identify the connected account.
  • Content-posting permission — the scopes video.upload and video.publish let the App upload videos that you have created to your own TikTok account, at your direction or on a schedule you configure.
  • YouTube account — where connected, the App uses Google/ YouTube APIs under permissions you grant to upload your videos and read your own analytics.

How we use it

Accessed data is used for one purpose only: to publish videos you create to your own connected accounts, and to read back your own performance statistics so the App can help you decide what to make next. We do not use it for advertising, profiling, or any unrelated purpose.

Storage and security

Authorization tokens are stored locally on the operator's own computer. The App has no central server that aggregates user data; information flows only between the operator's device and the platform APIs (TikTok, Google/YouTube) the operator connects. Tokens are kept only as long as needed to operate the App.

Sharing

We do not sell, rent, or share your information with third parties. Data is exchanged only with the platforms you explicitly connect, under their own terms.

Data retention & deletion

You can revoke the App's access at any time by disconnecting it within the App or by removing it from your TikTok / Google account's connected-apps settings. Doing so invalidates the stored tokens and ends all access. Deleting the App's local data folder removes any stored information.

Third-party services

Your use of TikTok and YouTube through the App is also governed by their respective privacy policies and terms of service.

Children

The App is not directed to children under 13 and does not knowingly collect information from them.

Changes

We may update this policy; material changes will be reflected by the "Last updated" date above.

Contact
